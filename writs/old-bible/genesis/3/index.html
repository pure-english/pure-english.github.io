    Genesis 3 - The fall

    1. Now the snake was craftier than any of the wild deer the Lord God had made. He said to the wife, ‘Did God truly say, “You must not eat from any tree in the leighton”?’
    2. The wife said to the snake, ‘We may eat ovet from the trees in the leighton,
    3. but God did say, “Thou must not eat ovet from the tree that is in the middle of the leighton, and thou must not touch it, or thou wilt dead.”’
    4. ‘Thou wilt not wissly dead,’ the snake said to the wife.
    5. ‘For God knows that when thou eatest from it thine eyes will6 be opened, and thou wilt be like God, knowing good and evil.’
    6. When the wife saw that the ovet of the tree was good for food and sightly, and also willsome for gaining wisdom, she nimmed some and ate it.
    7. Then the eyes of both of hem were opened, and hy understood that hy were naked; so hy sewed fike leaves together and made hames for hemselves.
    8. Then the were and his wife heard the swey of the Lord God as hy were walking in the leighton in the cool of the day, and hy hid from the Lord God among the trees of the leighton.
    9. But the Lord God chyed to the were, ‘Where art thou?’
    10. He answered, ‘I heard thee in the leighton, and I was scared since I was naked; so I hid.’
    11. And hy said, ‘Who told thee that thou wast naked? Hast thou eaten from the tree from which I bade you not to eat?’
    12. The were said, ‘The wife thou puttest here mid me – she yave me some ovet from the tree, and I ate it.’
    13. Then the Lord God said to the wife, ‘What is this thou hast done?’
      The wife said, ‘The snake misled me, and I ate.’
    14. So the Lord God said to the snake, ‘Since thou hast done this,
    15. 'Cursed art thou above all livestock
      and all wild deer!
      Thou wilt smow on thy belly
      and thou wilt eat dust
      all the days of thy life.
      And I will put foeship
      between thou and the wife,
      and between thine offspring1 and hers;
      he will crush2 thy head,
      and thou wilt strike his heel.'
    16. To the wife he said,
      ‘I will make thy warks in childbearing awfully swingeing;
      mid warkand swink thou wilt bear children.
      Thy craving will be for thy were,
      and he will lord over thee.’
    17. To Adam he said, ‘Since thou listenedst to thy wife and ate ovet from the tree about which I bade you, “You must not eat from it,”
      ‘Cursed is the ground thanks to thee;
      through warkand swink thou wilt eat food from it
      all the days of thy life.
    18. It will bring forth thorns and thistles for thee,
      and thou wilt eat the worts of the field.
    19. By the sweat of thy brow
      thou wilt eat thy food
      oth thou goest back to the ground,
      since from it thou wast nimmed;
      for dust thou art
      and to dust thou wilt go back.’
    20. Adam3 named his wife Ewe,4 as she would become the mother of all the living.
    21. The Lord God made weeds of hide for Adam and his wife and clothed hem.
    22. And the Lord God said, ‘The were hath now become like one of us, knowing good and evil. He must not be left to reach out his hand and nim also from the tree of life and eat, and live for ever.’
    23. So the Lord God outed him from the Leighton of Eden to work the ground from which he had been nimmed.
    24. After hy drove the were out, hy put on the east side5 of the Leighton of Eden cherubim and a leaming sword lixing7 back and forth to ward the way to the tree of life.

    1. or seed. ↩

    2. or strike. ↩

    3. or the were. ↩

    4. Ewe probably means living in the original Hebrew. ↩

    5. or put in front. ↩

    6. Could have used wilt, but I'm only using the archaic conjugations when paired with thou. ↩

    7. English word was "flashing". In the sense of a light flash, the word lixing is correct. In the sense of flaunting, the word touting would be used. ↩
